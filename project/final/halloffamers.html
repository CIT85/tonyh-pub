Quarterback Legends

Joe Montana

Joe Montana, a third round draft in 1979 was elected to the Hall of Fame on January 29, 2000. He played for 15 seasons and is a four time Super Bowl winner. Montana had an amazing pro football career and was also elected for 8 Pro Bowls throughout is career.

Steve Young

Steve Young was traded to the San Francisco 49ers in 1987, where he was the backup for Joe Montana. Young played pro football for 15 seasons and is a three time Super Bowl winner. He was elected for Pro Bowls 7 times and is known for being one of the most accurate passers in the league, as well as being a constant threat as a runner.

Wide Receiver Icons

Jerry Rice

Considered the greatest wide receiver of all time, the first round draft in 1985, Jerry Rice holds numerous NFL records. Rice played for 20 seasons and was voted for Pro Bowls 13 times. He also holds 3 Super Bowl wins and holds many records of his time. Some records he set at the time of his retirements includes, most career touchdowns(208), most career pass receptions(1,549), most season receiving yards gained (1,848 yards), and many more.
